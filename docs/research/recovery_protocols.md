## Recovery Timeline Framework

### Muscle Strain Recovery Durations

Based on sports medicine literature, muscle strain recovery follows these evidence-based timelines:

| Severity | Type | Duration | Description |
|----------|------|----------|-------------|
| **Mild** | Type I (Overexertion) | 5-8 days | Functional muscle disorder without macroscopic fiber tear |
| **Moderate** | Type III (Partial Tear) | 16-38 days (2-5 weeks) | Partial structural damage with fiber disruption |
| **Severe** | Complete Muscle Injury | 62 days (9 weeks) | (Sub)total tear or tendinous avulsion |

**Source:** Sports medicine research on muscle injury classification

| **Patellar Tendinitis** | 2-4 weeks | 6-12 weeks | 3-6 months |

## Recovery Phases

### Phase 1: Acute (0-5 days)

**Characteristics:**
- Immediate post-injury period
- Peak inflammation and pain
- Focus on protection and pain management

**Management:**
- RICE protocol (Rest, Ice, Compression, Elevation)
- Avoid aggravating activities
- Pain management (ice, NSAIDs if appropriate)
- Gentle range of motion within pain-free limits

**Check-in Frequency:** Daily self-assessment

**Expected Progress:**
- Pain reduction from initial levels
- Swelling stabilization or reduction
- Improved ability to perform basic movements

---

### Phase 2: Early-Stage Recovery (5-21 days)

**Characteristics:**
- Inflammation subsiding
- Tissue healing begins
- Gradual return to light activity

**Management:**
- Progressive loading within pain tolerance
- Mobility and flexibility work
- Light strengthening exercises
- Modified training (reduced volume/intensity)

**Check-in Frequency:** Every 3-5 days (twice weekly)

**Expected Progress:**
- 10-20% improvement per week in early phase
- Gradual increase in pain-free range of motion
- Ability to perform daily activities without significant limitation

---

### Phase 3: Advanced Recovery (21 days to 6-12 months)

**Characteristics:**
- Tissue remodeling and strengthening
- Progressive return to full activity
- Focus on preventing re-injury

**Management:**
- Sport-specific training progression
- Strength and conditioning
- Technique refinement
- Gradual return to pre-injury training loads

**Check-in Frequency:** Weekly

**Expected Progress:**
- Continued strength gains
- Return to sport-specific movements
- Minimal to no pain with activity

## Weekly Check-in Assessment Framework

### Assessment Questions

**1. Pain Level (0-10 scale)**
- Current pain at rest
- Pain with specific movements
- Pain during daily activities
- Pain during exercise

**2. Range of Motion Quality**
- Compared to uninjured side
- Pain-free ROM percentage
- Stiffness or catching sensations

**3. Activity Tolerance**
- What activities can you perform without pain?
- What activities cause pain or discomfort?
- Have you been able to increase activity level?

**4. New Symptoms**
- Any new pain locations?
- Swelling, bruising, or discoloration?
- Numbness, tingling, or weakness?

**5. Functional Capacity**
- Can you perform daily activities normally?
- Can you perform modified training?
- Can you perform sport-specific movements?

## Normal Progression Indicators

### Week-to-Week Improvement Benchmarks

**Early Phase (Weeks 1-3):**
- 10-20% reduction in pain per week
- Gradual increase in pain-free ROM
- Improved ability to perform daily activities

**Mid Phase (Weeks 3-6):**
- 5-15% improvement per week
- Return to light training with modifications
- Minimal pain with daily activities

**Late Phase (Weeks 6+):**
- Continued strength gains
- Progressive return to full training
- Minimal to no pain with most activities

## Warning Signs & Escalation Criteria

### Plateau Identification

**Concern if:**
- No improvement for 2-3 consecutive check-ins
- Pain level unchanged for 2+ weeks
- ROM not improving over 2+ weeks
- Unable to progress activity level

**Action:** Consider physical therapy consultation

### Worsening Symptoms

**Immediate Concern if:**
- Pain increasing instead of decreasing
- New swelling or bruising
- Increasing functional limitation
- New symptoms (numbness, tingling, weakness)

**Action:** Consult healthcare provider within 48 hours

## Recovery Progress Calculation

### Progress Score Formula

```
progress_score = (
  (pain_reduction * 0.4) +
  (rom_improvement * 0.3) +
  (functional_capacity * 0.2) +
  (activity_tolerance * 0.1)
)
```

**Where:**
- `pain_reduction` = (initial_pain - current_pain) / initial_pain
- `rom_improvement` = current_rom / target_rom
- `functional_capacity` = activities_possible / total_activities
- `activity_tolerance` = current_intensity / target_intensity

**Interpretation:**
- 0.0-0.3: Minimal progress (consider escalation)
- 0.3-0.6: Moderate progress (continue current plan)
- 0.6-0.8: Good progress (consider progression)
- 0.8-1.0: Excellent progress (near return-to-activity)
