## üèÉ RUNNING BADGES - Industry Standards

### **Nike Run Club (NRC) - The Gold Standard**

**Achievement System:**
- **Distance Milestones:** First run, 5K, 10K, Half Marathon (13.1mi), Marathon (26.2mi)
- **Total Distance:** 50km, 100km, 250km, 500km, 1000km milestones
- **Levels System:** 
  - Yellow (0-50km)
  - Orange (50-250km)
  - Green (250-500km)
  - Blue (500-1000km)
  - Purple (1000-2500km)
  - **Black Level (2500km+)** - Ultimate achievement
- **Consistency:** Weekly streak badges, monthly challenges
- **Social:** Share achievements, compete with friends

**Key Insight:** NRC uses **progressive levels** that give long-term goals beyond individual badges. Users work toward "Black Level" status.

---

### **Strava - Segment-Based Competition**

**Achievement System:**
- **KOM/QOM (King/Queen of the Mountain):** Fastest time on a segment
- **Course Records (CR):** Best time on verified routes
- **Local Legend:** Most runs on a segment in 90 days
- **Best Efforts:** Automatic PR tracking for:
  - 400m, 800m, 1K, 1 mile, 2 miles
  - 5K, 10K, 15K, 10 miles, 20K
  - Half marathon, 30K, Marathon, 50K
- **Monthly Challenges:** Distance/elevation goals with badges

**Key Insight:** Strava's **segment-based competition** creates micro-achievements. Every run can yield multiple PRs on different segments.

**What Works:**
- Automatic PR detection across 14 standard distances
- Competitive leaderboards (global, age group, gender)
- "Local Legend" rewards consistency on favorite routes

**What Doesn't:**
- Removed "Year KOMs" (users complained)
- Too many segments can be overwhelming
- GPS inaccuracies cause frustration

### **Running Speed Benchmarks (Real-World Data)**

**5K Times:**
- **Sub-30 minutes:** Beginner milestone (9:40/mile pace)
- **Sub-25 minutes:** Intermediate runner (8:03/mile pace)
- **Sub-20 minutes:** Advanced runner (6:26/mile pace)
- **Sub-18 minutes:** Elite/competitive (5:47/mile pace)

**10K Times:**
- **Sub-60 minutes:** Beginner milestone (9:40/mile pace)
- **Sub-50 minutes:** Intermediate runner (8:03/mile pace)
- **Sub-45 minutes:** Advanced runner (7:15/mile pace)
- **Sub-40 minutes:** Elite/competitive (6:26/mile pace)

**Mile Times:**
- **Sub-10 minutes:** Beginner milestone
- **Sub-8 minutes:** Intermediate runner
- **Sub-7 minutes:** Advanced runner
- **Sub-6 minutes:** Elite runner
- **Sub-5 minutes:** Extremely rare (Olympic-level)
- **Sub-4 minutes:** Fewer people than have summited Everest!

**Marathon Times:**
- **Sub-5 hours:** Completion milestone
- **Sub-4 hours:** Intermediate (9:09/mile pace)
- **Sub-3:30:** Advanced (8:00/mile pace)
- **Sub-3 hours:** Elite (6:52/mile pace) - Top 1% of marathoners

**Key Insight:** Speed badges should have **realistic tiers**. Sub-5 minute mile is too elite for most users.

### **Peloton - Milestone-Based System**

**Achievement System:**
- **Workout Count:** 5, 10, 25, 50, 100, 250, 500, 750, 1000+ workouts
- **Discipline-Specific:** Separate milestones for cycling, strength, yoga, running

### **JEFIT & Strong - Powerlifting Focus**

**Achievement System:**
- **Workout Streaks:** 7, 30, 60, 100, 365 days
- **Total Volume:** Tonnage milestones (100K, 500K, 1M, 5M lbs)
- **PR Tracking:** Automatic 1RM calculation and PR detection
- **Exercise Mastery:** Badges for hitting specific lift milestones
- **Gamification:** XP points, levels, medals for categories (Strength, Endurance, Stamina)

**Key Insight:** Strength apps focus on **volume (tonnage)** and **PR progression** rather than just workout count.

## üî• STREAK BADGES - Industry Standards

### **Duolingo - The Streak Master**

**Achievement System:**
- **Daily Streaks:** 7, 14, 30, 50, 100, 365, 500, 1000+ days
- **Streak Freeze:** Allows 1 missed day without breaking streak (Premium)
- **Streak Society:** Exclusive club for 365+ day streaks
- **Badges:** Unlock at 7, 30, 100, 365 days

**Key Insight:** Duolingo's success comes from **streak protection** (freeze) and **social pressure** (friends can see your streak).

**What Works:**
- Streak freeze reduces anxiety
- Daily reminders at optimal times
- Celebration animations when hitting milestones
- Leaderboards with friends

**What Doesn't:**
- Streaks can become stressful (fear of breaking)
- Some users feel "trapped" by long streaks

---

### **Fitness App Streak Standards**

**Common Streak Milestones:**
- **7 days:** First week (hook users)
- **30 days:** One month (habit formation)
- **60 days:** Two months
- **100 days:** Major milestone (rare achievement)
- **365 days:** One year (elite consistency)

**Weekly Streaks (Alternative to Daily):**
- **4 weeks:** One month
- **12 weeks:** Three months
- **26 weeks:** Six months
- **52 weeks:** One year

**Key Insight:** **Weekly streaks** are more sustainable than daily streaks for fitness. Peloton and NRC both use weekly consistency badges.

## üìä GAMIFICATION BEST PRACTICES

### **What Works:**

1. **Progressive Difficulty:**
   - Early milestones close together (5, 10, 25 workouts)
   - Later milestones spread out (250, 500, 1000 workouts)
   - Keeps users engaged at all levels

2. **Multiple Achievement Paths:**
   - Distance runners get distance badges
   - Speed runners get pace badges
   - Consistent runners get streak badges
   - Everyone finds something to chase

3. **Automatic Detection:**
   - No manual claiming required
   - Instant gratification when earned
   - Celebration animations

4. **Social Sharing:**
   - Share badges to Instagram/Facebook
   - Compete with friends
   - Public leaderboards

5. **Visual Design:**
   - Colorful, distinct badge designs
   - Progress bars toward next badge
   - "Locked" badges show what's possible

### **What Doesn't Work:**

1. **Too Many Badges:**
   - Overwhelming for new users
   - Dilutes achievement value

2. **Unrealistic Milestones:**
   - Sub-5 minute mile (too elite)
   - 1000-day streak (too stressful)

3. **Removing Achievements:**
   - Strava removed "Year KOMs" - users complained
   - Never take away earned badges

4. **Hidden Requirements:**
   - Users should know what they're working toward
   - Show progress toward unearned badges

## ‚úÖ FINAL RECOMMENDATIONS

### **Total Badge Count: ~90 badges**

**Distribution:**
- 30 Strength Training badges

### **Key Design Principles:**

5. **Automatic Detection:** No manual claiming
6. **Visual Progress:** Show progress toward next badge
7. **Social Sharing:** Share achievements to social media
8. **Celebration:** Animations, notifications, confetti when earned

### **Differentiation from Competitors:**

‚úÖ **Hybrid badges** (strength + running) - No competitor does this!

‚úÖ **Weekly consistency** - More sustainable than daily streaks
